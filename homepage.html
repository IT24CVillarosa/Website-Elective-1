Welcome to Philosophia

Explore the great minds and thoughts that shaped the world of philosophy.

Socrates

School: Socratic Method, Ethics

Known for his unique approach to philosophy, focusing on questioning and dialogue.

Plato

School: Idealism, Theory of Forms

Plato believed in a realm of abstract Forms and ideals, which underpin our physical reality.

Aristotle

School: Empiricism, Logic

Aristotle’s work laid the foundation for much of Western science and philosophy.

Confucius

School: Confucianism, Ethics

Confucius emphasized moral values, respect, and harmony in society.

René Descartes

School: Rationalism, Dualism

Known for "I think, therefore I am," Descartes explored the nature of knowledge and existence.

David Hume

School: Empiricism, Skepticism

Hume questioned human knowledge, belief, and the nature of causation.

Immanuel Kant

School: Idealism, Ethics

Kant developed the concept of the categorical imperative and influenced ethics and epistemology.

Friedrich Nietzsche

School: Existentialism, Nihilism

Nietzsche explored individualism, morality, and the concept of the "Übermensch."

Martin Heidegger

School: Existentialism, Phenomenology

Heidegger focused on the nature of Being and human existence.

Jean-Paul Sartre

School: Existentialism

Sartre championed freedom, responsibility, and existentialism's notion of "existence precedes essence."

Arthur Schopenhauer

School: Pessimism, Metaphysics

Schopenhauer emphasized the primacy of the "will" in human motivation and the nature of suffering.

Bertrand Russell

School: Analytic Philosophy, Logic

Russell made significant contributions to logic, mathematics, and the philosophy of language.
